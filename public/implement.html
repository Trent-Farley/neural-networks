Implementation

The amount of information on the implementation of machine networks is staggering. From material science to recognizing handwriting, the amount of increasing data that can be processed is staggering. To implement something as researched as this, there has to be a bit of calculus and higher level math. Both of these areas (Material science and image recognition) are researched heavily and are linked under the sources page.

How to implement a neural network

  1. Create an end goal
  2. Initialize weight of node (starting point)
  3. Trim the failed nodes from the structure
  4. Repeat until goal is accomplished

As discussed by the theory of neural networks, the great thing about computers is that they do not care how many times a task needs to be repeated for it to finish. As humans, we think of the repetitive chores as a burden and try to avoid them. Computers, however, are very good at this and can reiterate and complete a task in a mere instant. It is not unheard of a neural network to hit 200 generations of nodes still working on a problem.
